[![](/assets/images/peterlubbers.jpeg "Peter Lubbers")](/assets/images/peterlubbers.jpeg)Greetings from [DevCon5 NYC](http://www.html5report.com/conference/newyork/date-time.aspx). Our morning starts with [Peter Lubbers](http://www.amazon.com/Pro-HTML5-Programming-Application-Development/dp/1430227907 "Author of \"Pro HTML5 Programming\"") giving an HTML5 overview. He's reminding us about some important fundamentals:
\* Web Origins (see [RFC 6454](http://www.ietf.org/rfc/rfc6454.txt "RFC 6454, The Web Origin Concept")) are everywhere. You need to understand not only how they regulate JavaScript execution, [XMLHttpRequests](http://www.w3.org/TR/XMLHttpRequest/), [LocalStorage](https://developer.mozilla.org/en/DOM/Storage "MDN on localStorage"). Be sure to understand and specify [Cross-Origin Resource Sharing (CORS)](http://www.w3.org/TR/cors/ "W3C on CORS") policy.
\* Investigate [Web Intents](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Web_Intents "Wikipedia on Web Intents") as a way to better integrate Apps and avoid clunky workflows and/or reimplementations. (note: more standardization is needed here)
\* Learn about real-time connectivity options, including [cross-document messaging using postMessage](https://developer.mozilla.org/en/DOM/window.postMessage), [XMLHttpRequest level 2](http://www.w3.org/TR/XMLHttpRequest/), [Server-sent Events](http://www.w3.org/TR/eventsource/), [WebSockets](https://developer.mozilla.org/en/WebSockets/), and even [WebRTC](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WebRTC).
\* Learn about browser compatibility at [caniuse.com](http://caniuse.com "http://caniuse.com"), [mobilehtml5.org](http://mobilehtml5.org "http://mobilehtml5.org"), and at [MDN](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/)
\* Be inspired by great demos of WebSockets, WebGL at [http://kaazing.com/demos](http://kaazing.com/demos "Kaazing Demos")
